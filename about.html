About Me

Who I am

I'm a 14-year old geek living in Falls Church, Virginia. I love photography, hiking, writing, cycling, listening to music, making obscure geeky references, and anything with technology. In Chinese I'm called 包天佑. It means Blessed/Protected by Heaven, although Google Translate may tell you otherwise.

Other than my interest in technology, I'm a somewhat normal teenager (except without the rebellion and tattoos). I go to Mary Ellen Henderson Middle School. You can read about my religious thoughts here.

What I've done

Throughout my life I've been interested in technology, and have gained experience in many areas of it, including CAD, sound, video, TV broadcast directing, game design, app development, robotics, back-end, photography & graphic design, writing/blogging & electronics and hardware, web, and more.

I've volunteered twice at New Day, a Chinese foster home for orphans with special needs. I've also been to Malaysia and lived in Chicago for four years. In 8th grade I achieved the Gold award in Technology Student Association, and also directed my school's Morning Announcements. I competed in the FIRST Lego Robotics challenge four times during Middle School.

What I do

I'm mainly interested in photography, web design, and tech/hardware repair & support.

In my free time I do photography or write about social issues, tech, and my brain's random musings on my blog. I also enjoy biking, backpacking, and climbing (rock or otherwise), and playing the trumpet. I do occasional drawing. I love listening to nearly all genres of music, including rock, alternative, electronic, and occasionally pop. If you like, you can listen to a playlist of all my favorite music here.

What I'm going to do

Next year, I'll start High School at George Mason, where I'll become proficient in numerous subjects whilst focusing in on Computer Science-related subjects. I'll also participate in the school's FIRST Robotics club, where I plan to learn a lot about robots and applying technology in the real world. After High School, I'm going to apply to Stanford, MIT, or the University of Michigan. Then I want to work at Google, or a web agency such as Viget or Interactive Strategies. Later in life I plan to be a tech teacher and bring new teaching methods to elementary/Middle School Computer Science education. That is, assuming human teachers are not yet obsolete.
